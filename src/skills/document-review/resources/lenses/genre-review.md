# Genre Review Lens

Assess whether the artifact fulfills the declared genre and reader contract without requiring the reader to reconstruct the Case.

For an RFC decision brief, verify the decision or ask appears first, scope is bounded, supporting evidence and trade-offs are sufficient, risks and open gaps are visible, and the requested action is explicit. When policy is already accepted, verify that the artifact is a decision record with no Case-backed action. An externally supplied review instruction may be recorded as context, but cannot be reviewed as Case-supported or presented as publication-ready authority. Flag a genre failure when a reader must reconstruct the recommendation from sources or when background crowds out the action.

For an evidence-heavy research report, verify that it states the reader action, presents a qualified conclusion, separates observation from accepted policy, exposes decisive and conflicting evidence, gives material limitations appropriate prominence, and ends with a usable decision boundary. Flag chronology dumps, unqualified recommendations, buried caveats, and evidence lists that never explain why they matter.

For a blog post, verify that the opening uses an effective hook grounded in the actual subject; establishes the reader problem, observation, tension, question, or promise without requiring privileged context; and gives the reader enough orientation to understand what follows. Verify that the practical takeaway and intended audience are clear; supporting examples are introduced only after their relationship to the subject is legible; accessible attribution does not expose private working context; material uncertainty is visible; and the close gives the promised bounded action or perspective. Flag an in-medias-res opening whose implied world is not yet grounded, a hook that promises a different article, a source inventory masquerading as narrative, a takeaway stronger than its support, or a conclusion that requires inaccessible evidence to understand.

Do not require a generic RFC outline. Recommend only changes that make the declared reader action possible without adding Case meaning. Record each finding with severity, locator, reader impact, and a proposed non-semantic edit when one exists. A repair that alters authority, confidence, scope, evidence, or reader action must route to `document-reconcile`.
