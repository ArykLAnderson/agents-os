# Fresh Reader Comprehension Lens

Simulate the named reader using only a frozen named artifact revision and its stated reader contract, not the Case, trace, sources, review notes, or author instructions. Record the artifact locator or digest and the excluded inputs so the result can be inspected as isolated comprehension evidence. This is comprehension evidence, not stakeholder approval, authority, or a substitute for fidelity review.

Record concise answers to these prompts:

1. After the opening, what does the reader believe the artifact is about, why does it matter, and what will the artifact help them understand or do?
2. Can the reader construct a closed mental model of the subject without privileged author context: relevant actors, inputs, persistent artifacts or state, key relationships, outputs, lifecycle boundaries, and human authority?
3. Which concepts does the artifact rely on before grounding them? For each, name the first anchor that assumes it and the earlier anchor that should have introduced it.
4. What action is the reader being asked to take or avoid?
5. What is the recommendation, and how qualified is it?
6. Which observation supports the recommendation, and who selected the policy direction?
7. What contradictory or weak-authority claim should not be treated as current policy?
8. What limitation or unresolved question constrains the action?
9. Retell the artifact's argument in ordinary language without reusing its section labels. Where does that retelling become uncertain, mechanical, or impossible?
10. Identify the first sentence in every passage where orientation or confidence drops. Is the cause an ungrounded concept, missing relationship, abrupt transition, framework jargon, or unnatural adjacency between otherwise correct statements?
11. Does the prose sound like one writer developing an idea, or like supported points assembled to satisfy an accounting checklist? Cite the strongest example of each.

Record a misunderstanding when the hook points at a broader or different subject than the artifact delivers, the artifact relies on an implied world the reader cannot map, a concept is used before it is grounded, the artifact makes an unsupported decision, hides a caveat needed for the action, blurs evidence with authority, or leaves the next step unclear. Treat lingering uncertainty about what exists, how parts relate, or where the system boundary closes as a comprehension failure even when each individual sentence is clear. State the simulation's confidence limit: it cannot establish real reader comprehension, approval, or operational acceptance.

Treat unnatural flow as a finding even when no factual error exists. Look for paragraphs whose sentences share a Case topic but not a reader-visible progression, transitions that merely announce the next requirement, repeated definitions inserted to satisfy coverage, and examples that interrupt rather than carry the explanation. Recommend section- or paragraph-level reshaping when sentence edits cannot restore a coherent voice.

When a normal artifact entrypoint selects among revisions, use that entrypoint rather than a hand-selected revision. Also record whether the reader can recover the current safe artifact, its status, pinned snapshot set, and the support and authority for its action. Treat a stale baseline presented as current as a misunderstanding.
Also record whether the reader can identify the current direction or unresolved decision, material risk or caveat, and next owner or approval boundary. Associate each plausible misunderstanding with the artifact anchor that caused it. Keep the result short: outcome, confidence, answers, misunderstanding signals, and the smallest author-facing change needed.
