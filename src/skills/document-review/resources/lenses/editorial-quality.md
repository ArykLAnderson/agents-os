# Editorial Quality Lens

Check clarity, concision, hierarchy, and emphasis after fidelity and genre findings are known. Safe edits may remove repetition, improve labels, shorten prose, or reorder already-equivalent material. Preserve all material caveats, authority distinctions, confidence limits, and decision boundaries.

Also check grounding and closure. Build a short concept-dependency map from the artifact: concepts assumed from the audience, concepts introduced by each section, and later sections that rely on them. Flag a term, example, actor, artifact, or relationship used before its concept is available. For explanatory system writing, verify that the reader can identify the system boundary, actors, inputs, persistent state, transformations, outputs, and authority boundaries. Clear sentences do not compensate for a loosely bound subject or an unclosed mental model.

Check prose flow independently from semantic coverage. Read paragraph transitions and sentence adjacency without consulting the Case-entry sequence. Flag prose that preserves ledger order, gives each requirement its own sentence, repeats a subject only to attach the next fact, or uses generic connective language to hide that two ideas have no reader-visible relationship. Prefer rebuilding a paragraph around one controlling thought over polishing every accounted-for sentence in place.

Review the shaped prose as a whole before target formatting. The preferred input is the current reader-facing semantic artifact without Case, trace, selection manifest, prior findings, or author rationale. Use those materials only in a separate fidelity pass.

Evaluate the opening as an editorial device, not merely as attractive prose. Record what the hook makes the reader expect, where the article fulfills that expectation, and whether the hook grounds or obscures the actual subject. Do not require one universal rhetorical device; judge the selected device by orientation, relevance, and payoff.

Escalate rather than automatically edit when a proposed change would alter trace coverage, scope, evidence, authority, confidence, recommendation strength, or reader action. Do not collapse alternatives, remove uncertainty, strengthen modality, or reword a decision such that the trace assertion changes. Return concrete anchor-level edits, record the before/after effect for every material-looking edit, and identify changes that require trace or Case review.
