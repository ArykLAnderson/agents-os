# Presentation Quality Lens

Inspect the declared reader-facing representation after semantic review. Check heading hierarchy, table readability, locator stability, plain-language labels, and whether important limitations or decision asks are visible without depending on collapsed, hover-only, or target-specific disclosure.

For every meaning-bearing visual, inspect it at its article position and expected desktop and narrow reading sizes. Check whether it answers the visual anchor's reader question, makes the takeaway dominant, duplicates adjacent prose, exposes implementation accounting, or falsely implies pipeline order, causality, completeness, approval, or mandatory action. Consider decomposition, distribution, semantic HTML, a table, cards, prose, or removal.

Treat a caption that must correct the visual's dominant reading as a blocking finding. Treat unreadable narrow-view image text as a route failure, not as a limitation solved by alt text. The textual equivalent must preserve meaning without rescuing a confusing visual.

Record target-specific issues separately from semantic findings. Do not claim visual rendering, accessibility conformance, or device validation unless it was actually performed.
