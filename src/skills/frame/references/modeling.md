# Modeling

Clarify the language and behavior needed by the outcome:

- concepts, terminology, actors, and responsibilities;
- relationships, states, transitions, and lifecycles;
- rules, invariants, authority boundaries, and exceptions;
- examples, counterexamples, and competing interpretations.

Ground models in evidence and relevant Cases. Preserve competing meanings until applicable authority resolves them. Use terminology-focused Cases when the vocabulary is independently reusable. Propose coherent changes through Case reconciliation rather than mutating an assumed glossary or requiring a project-specific documentation convention.

Modeling completes when the concepts needed by the current outcome are precise enough to reason about and unresolved interpretations are visible.
