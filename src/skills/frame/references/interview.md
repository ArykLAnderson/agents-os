# Interview

Resolve one bounded human-knowledge objective. Research facts first, then ask only for knowledge, authority, preference, or judgment the human must supply.

Probe selectively through these gap lenses:

- Meaning
- Evidence
- Authority
- Consistency
- Consequence
- Counterfactual
- Durability
- Attachment
- Scope

Ask in dependency order. Default to one consequential question at a time. Group at most three only when each is short, concrete, answerable in a phrase, shares context, and has low cognitive load. Ask rationale or consequential trade-offs alone.

Use a **delta-first decision card** whose length is proportional to the difficulty and consequence of the choice:

- **Context:** give enough plain-language orientation to resume this decision after an attention switch; name the current design area and what this choice controls.
- **Meaning:** define any term whose interpretation materially affects the choice. Omit this section when ordinary language is sufficient.
- **Recommendation:** state the proposed choice directly. Surface only the precise new or corrected meaning that sharpens the human's position; omit broad restatement and structured echo.
- **Why / consequences:** list the few decisive effects in brief bullets. Include disagreement, risk, or hidden cost when material.
- **Decision:** ask one bounded question whose visible proposition is the full scope of what confirmation accepts.

Keep deeper modeling and rationale in Frame/Case state. Offer or provide a drill-down when requested. Compress routine, reversible choices; expand hard-to-reverse, high-risk, contested, unfamiliar, or context-heavy choices enough to support judgment. Split depth across successive decisions rather than presenting a mini-RFC.

Test answers against evidence and prior accepted meaning. Confirmation accepts only the visible proposition, never unstated rationale or implications. At natural boundaries, present a concise checkpoint of exact accepted propositions so accidental agreement can be corrected. When options cannot be distinguished, recommend the next discriminating evidence, question, deferral, or reframing neutrally.

Treat the bounded interview as one operation. Keep accepted answers in conversational working state and ask the next question without writing Frame or Case files after every answer. The natural persistence boundary is when the interview objective is resolved, blocked, deliberately deferred, preserved as contested, the human pauses or redirects, compaction/handoff risk requires a checkpoint, or the conversation leaves the interview for research, prototyping, deliberation, or another operation. Before switching operations or following the new direction, reconcile the accepted batch and dispositions so an abandoned thread does not lose its decisions. Persist an individual answer immediately only when the human requests it or a consequential external effect requires a durable prerequisite.

Continue until that boundary. Put adjacent concerns on the Discovery Map rather than expanding the interview.
