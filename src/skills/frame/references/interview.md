# Interview

Resolve one bounded human-knowledge objective. Research facts first, then ask only for knowledge, authority, preference, or judgment the human must supply.

Probe selectively through these gap lenses:

- Meaning
- Evidence
- Authority
- Consistency
- Consequence
- Counterfactual
- Durability
- Attachment
- Scope

Ask in dependency order. Group at most three questions when each is short, concrete, answerable in a phrase or short sentence, shares context, and has low cognitive load. Ask rationale or consequential trade-offs alone.

Test answers against evidence and prior accepted meaning. Give the strongest supported recommendation. When options cannot be distinguished, recommend the next discriminating evidence, question, deferral, or reframing neutrally.

Continue until the objective is resolved, blocked, deliberately deferred, or preserved as contested. Put adjacent concerns on the Discovery Map rather than expanding the interview.
