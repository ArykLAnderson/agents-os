# Review Briefing Strategy

Use for a reader who already has enough domain context to assess one bounded decision, change, or handoff.

## Reader Journey

1. Open with the review ask and decision boundary.
2. Show the current conclusion or proposed delta before background.
3. Put evidence beside the claim it supports; make evidence distinct from authority.
4. Surface material risk, limitation, stale baseline, and omission before the final action.
5. End with the smallest explicit reader action and escalation boundary.

## Anchors And Fit

Use stable anchors for `review-ask`, `change-or-conclusion`, `evidence`, `limitations`, and `decision-boundary`. Tables and visuals need their own anchors when they carry material meaning.

This strategy does not fit when a reader cannot understand the nouns, lifecycle, or relationships used in the review ask. Use `mental-model` first in that case. Do not hide unresolved requirements in a review checklist.
