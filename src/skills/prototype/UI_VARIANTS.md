# UI Variant Prototypes

Use this branch when the question is open-ended and interaction or information structure cannot be settled from prose alone.

Create three interactive variants by default. Each variant must express a different structural hypothesis relevant to the question, such as navigation model, information hierarchy, task flow, disclosure strategy, or interaction pattern. Color, typography, spacing, and decoration alone do not make variants structurally distinct.

Keep shared content and evaluation conditions comparable. Make switching among variants quick and preserve enough instrumentation or observation prompts to compare them against the same question.

Do not manufacture a winner. Report what evaluators observed, which criteria favored each variant, where evidence conflicted, and what remained untested. For open-ended exploration, always provide three variants. A more narrowly bounded question may use fewer only when the result names the excluded structural hypotheses and explains why they are not credible alternatives.
