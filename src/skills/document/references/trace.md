# Trace

Map consequential semantic units to understandable Case support.

For each decision, requirement, quantitative claim, causal claim, risk, recommendation, quotation, and material caveat, record:

- document location or semantic-unit ID;
- supporting Case entry or source pinpoint;
- internal support locator and, when the artifact cites evidence for readers, an audience-accessible source or approved substitute;
- whether the text is direct support, synthesis, or inference;
- contested or provisional status;
- affected visual anchor or representation;
- staleness condition where relevant.

Trace coverage is risk-weighted, not sentence-counted. Account for selected material as represented, omitted, or deferred, and treat an unaccounted material omission as a blocker. Inspect unsupported connective reasoning and claims whose wording is stronger than their support. Internal Case paths and IDs are not reader-facing citations. When no audience-accessible source or approved substitute exists, qualify or omit the claim and record the access limitation. When pinned Case meaning changes, identify affected semantic units, visuals, findings, representations, and acceptance before revising.

Compare later Case meaning against the pinned state. Mark a unit stale only when accepted meaning changes its assertion, authority, scope, support, or reader action; otherwise mark it unchanged or review-needed. Preserve the original pin. A stale unit requires a new trace revision and affected-lens review before the revised artifact can be accepted.

Visuals inherit the same support obligations as prose. Trace labels, values, topology, chronology, and emphasized relationships; generated decoration must not imply unsupported facts.

Trace completes when every consequential unit is supported or visibly qualified and known Case changes can be mapped to affected artifact state.
