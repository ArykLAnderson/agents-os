# Compose

Choose one primary genre and satisfy its semantic obligations. Genre controls what the artifact must establish, not its visual template.

- **RFC:** context, problem, goals and non-goals, constraints, proposed design, alternatives, consequences, risks, rollout, operations, security/privacy, unresolved decisions.
- **PRD:** user or business problem, affected users, desired outcomes, scope, requirements, experience, success measures, dependencies, risks, rollout, unresolved product judgment.
- **Research report:** question, scope, method, evidence, findings, competing explanations, limitations, implications, and recommended further inquiry.
- **Change brief:** change boundary, motivation, before/after behavior, affected surfaces, reviewer map, risk, verification evidence, rollout, and known omissions.
- **Implementation report:** intended design, implemented behavior, architecture and key flows, deviations, evidence, operational implications, residual risks, and next decisions.
- **Explanation document:** reader's starting model, core mechanism, grounded examples, boundaries, failure modes, and a usable resulting mental model.
- **Blog post:** reader promise, motivating tension, evidence-backed narrative, concrete examples, implications, caveats, and a resonant close without overstating claims.

Build a loose semantic basis from the pinned Cases before polishing prose. Record selected material as represented, omitted, or deferred; for material omissions, record the reason and effect on the intended reader action. Every consequential claim must be supported, visibly qualified, or represented as a gap. Preserve contested meaning and distinguish Case evidence from document interpretation.

Composition completes when the genre obligations and intended reader action are represented in the semantic basis without unsupported connective claims.
