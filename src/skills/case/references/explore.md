# Explore

Retrieve and explain established knowledge while preserving scope, support, authority, disagreement, and supersession.

1. Search titles, summaries, aliases, facets, relationships, maps, and indexes.
2. Load only likely Cases, then follow precise entry, source, artifact, and relationship references as needed.
3. Answer from current Case meaning and identify material limitations or unresolved disagreement.
4. Report when the available Cases do not answer the question.

Explore may regenerate indexes, backlinks, resolvable references, and mechanically supported map membership. It does not conduct broad external research or invent classifications, tags, relationships, or projection Cases. Use Frame when fresh knowledge is required.
