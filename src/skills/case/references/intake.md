# Intake

Intake cohesive reusable meaning whose purpose and interpretation are sufficiently understood, whether supplied initially or established during Frame discovery. The containing Frame may remain active or contested; only the candidate Case meaning needs a coherent independent boundary. Use `provisional` or `contested` classification rather than postponing stable subordinate meaning. Defer only when the semantic boundary itself is too unclear to state independently.

1. Confirm the source purpose and intended reusable meaning are clear.
2. Resolve exactly one workspace authority through the [persistence procedure](persistence.md). Use its typed search/resolve and `case.read` operations for narrow overlap and identity checks; do not inspect or write Case files directly.
3. Select an existing Case or assemble a new complete typed Case whose subject, authority, lifecycle, retrieval context, and distribution boundary fit.
4. Register each independently identifiable source and its examination objective. Preserve its stable ID, locator, visibility, provenance, and retained-evidence references in the typed aggregate.
5. Extract purpose-relevant semantic units with stable IDs and human-readable labels, initial classifications, support, authority, inference disclosure, relationships, and private visibility.
6. Run the full validation checklist before completing the batch. Repair only derived or mechanical defects automatically; leave semantic defects explicit.
7. Invoke `case.create` for a new identity or, after an exact typed read, `case.commit_revision` for an existing identity. Submit the complete aggregate, provenance, commit basis, and selected authority's concurrency token; never use a direct Markdown write, fallback, or second authority.

Perform only narrow lookups needed for identity, provenance, authority, or obvious overlap. Use Frame when purpose, interpretation, trade-offs, or consequential decisions are unclear. Cases condense reusable meaning and cite retained evidence; do not copy research reports, transcripts, operation logs, or other detailed evidence wholesale when a source or Artifact preserves them better.

Return a concise disposition receipt to the calling Frame: Cases created or reconciled, entry IDs and classifications affected, retained evidence or Artifact references, material No Case results and reasons, and any candidate meaning still pending because its boundary remains unclear.

A projection Case may synthesize explicitly selected Case entries. Record the exact `case:<id>#CK-<id>` inputs in a `## Projection Basis` section and cite the relevant basis entries from every derived entry. It remains an ordinary Case with its own purpose, scope, authority, support, and classifications.
