# Review

Review challenges the Route; it does not own strategy or acceptance authority.

Before applying feedback, classify it:

- **Conformance repair:** Route contradicts accepted Blueprint/behavior or canonical Route guidance.
- **Evidence request:** improves proof for an already accepted Route obligation.
- **Route proposal:** changes strategy, Leg boundaries/order, dependencies, migration, compatibility, rollback, cleanup, or evidence semantics.
- **Architecture finding:** responsibility or Contract is missing/wrong; return to Blueprint.
- **Behavior finding:** accepted intent is missing/contradictory; return to Frame/human reconciliation.
- **Governance proposal:** changes package membership, completion criteria, proof protocols, or review gates; keep advisory until separately accepted.
- **Mistaken/out of scope:** reject with reason.

Reviewer labels such as `blocker` and repeated reviewer agreement do not confer authority. A later reviewer cannot bootstrap an unaccepted mechanism by validating it.

Challenge:

- whether Legs are genuinely vertical and independently verifiable;
- whether intermediate states are coherent and operable;
- whether edges name real prerequisites;
- whether transition coverage includes data, compatibility, operations, cleanup, and external seams;
- whether early Legs retire the most consequential uncertainty;
- whether Deliver would need to invent architecture, strategy, or acceptance evidence;
- whether deferred and temporary work has honest dispositions.

Retain review findings and their destinations. Do not rewrite the Route after every review comment; reconcile a coherent accepted correction batch at a natural boundary.
