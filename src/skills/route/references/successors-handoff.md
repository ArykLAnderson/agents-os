# Successors And Execution Handoff

## Same-Map Successor

Any semantic change to strategy, Feature/Leg/WI boundaries, prerequisites, convergence, migration/compatibility, evidence/E2E/security obligations, cleanup, invalidators, or transition assumptions requires a complete successor candidate and fresh exact acceptance. Do not patch an accepted body or Decision.

Preserve the stable `FM-*` identity when the governing Blueprint identity and bounded destination remain fundamentally the same. The successor names its exact predecessor Decision, reason/evidence, changed and preserved meaning, downstream/cross-Map impact, evidence reuse/revalidation, and affected execution. Explicitly enumerate every changed, removed, rehomed, or invalidated Feature, Leg, Work Item, edge, temporary mechanism, and deferral with disposition and predecessor/local-label reference; cover the unchanged remainder by exact reference without per-record ceremony.

The new immutable Decision declares its predecessor, superseding reason, and current effect. It becomes current semantic authority at Decision recording even if mechanical child/body projection remains partial. The predecessor and source evidence remain visible. Existing implementation is evidence/salvage only and must re-earn inclusion; Atlas does not stop or terminate execution itself.

Locator/format repair, derived-view refresh, or completion of exact authorized projection does not need a successor when meaning cannot change. If reasonable readers could disagree, return `semantic_change_uncertain` and require complete Route presentation/acceptance.

A new Map identity is reserved for a human/Blueprint disposition that fundamentally changes governing Blueprint identity or destination through split, combination, or replacement. The disposition names old/new Maps, relationship, ownership, retained history, and downstream reconciliation. Live legacy migration and the concrete threshold decision remain separate accepted work; Publisher does not decide them.

## Drift And Currentness

Distinguish:

- exact bound identity/bytes/authority unresolvable or changed — fail closed for recording and refuse ambiguous projection/handoff;
- exact binding unchanged but a newer revision exists — preserve exact projection, expose impact as stale/unknown, and never rebind silently;
- an accepted invalidator is verifiably triggered — preserve history/projection where unambiguous but suppress affected prerequisite/evidence/readiness claims; factual status may follow the already accepted rule, while semantic change requires a successor;
- expected predecessor changes before successor Decision — candidate is stale and must be fully re-presented.

Consumers cite the Decision they consumed and compare it with the current Map on admission, resume, dependency frontiers, effectful gates, and result. They stop or reconcile under separately accepted execution authority and never silently follow `latest`.

## Typed Execution Handoff

Export from the exact current Map Decision as:

- `HandoffReady` — complete planning projection and required live factual checks resolved non-blocking from authenticated/scoped authoritative sources; still not dispatch/readiness/effect authority;
- `HandoffWithLimitations` — successful plan export with typed non-readiness limits such as unknown/unsatisfied/stale prerequisite, invalid/unknown evidence, newer-revision impact, triggered invalidator, or unavailable independence;
- `HandoffRefusal` — incomplete/ambiguous publication, historical requested Decision, accepted Decision/projection conflict, unresolvable governing binding, or an attempt to make export dispatch/effect change.

The handoff contains Atlas/Map/current Decision locators; exact Blueprint bindings/coverage; Feature, Leg, WI identity/ownership/local-label mappings and Contracts; direct prerequisites and convergence owners; strategy, transition/migration/compatibility/cleanup, rejected alternatives, and invalidators; accepted proof/E2E/security profile; qualified Evidence References; authoritative source locators and live resolution/provenance/integrity results; publication status, successor impact, limitations, and explicit absent implementation/effect/PR/merge/deploy authorities.

It is a bounded projection of planning authority—not a queue, operational graph, source baseline lock, dispatch order, readiness verdict, or execution record. Execution consumers may add repository baseline, worktree, branch, overlap, and effect details from their proper owners, but cannot change accepted behavior, Blueprint Contracts, ownership, prerequisites, convergence, or proof allocation.

The installed `software-implementation` skill remains a generic coordinator with known Atlas-handoff conformance gaps. Until separately adapted, do not claim that passing it a summary preserves typed handoff limitations, stable Map/Feature/Leg/WI identities, accepted convergence/proof allocation, or successor-currentness.
