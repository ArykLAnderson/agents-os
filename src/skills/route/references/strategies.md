# Realization Strategies

Compare at least two materially different strategies when transition shape affects risk, evidence, reversibility, user impact, temporary complexity, or cost.

Triggers include:

- live-data or authority migration;
- multiple materially different consumers;
- compatibility windows or mixed versions;
- infrastructure/deployment changes;
- costly rollback or abandonment;
- external integrations or uncertain platforms;
- major responsibility movement/refactoring;
- flags, adapters, parallel systems, or cutovers.

Compare each strategy by:

- earliest meaningful integrated evidence;
- Leg verticality and Blueprint fidelity;
- migration, compatibility, and rollback safety;
- coherent intermediate states;
- temporary complexity and cleanup burden;
- operational and user-visible impact;
- parallelism, critical path, and convergence seams;
- abandonment cost;
- risk retired per Leg.

## Risk-First Adaptive Frontiers For Deep Chains

For a deeply nested realization graph, choose evidence checkpoints adaptively around the highest-risk unproven seam rather than imposing a fixed layer width or stride. Use the accepted Blueprint qualities, current terrain, evidence gaps, and strategy comparison dimensions above. When credible frontier choices imply a consequential trade-off not resolved by accepted authority, retain them as alternatives or return the choice for human judgment rather than inventing a weighting rule.

Select the smallest connected graph slice that exercises the seam from its producer through its real immediate consumer and includes the context required to make the claim truthful. A single producer-consumer crossing may be sufficient. The dependency graph still governs what can execute: when prerequisites are absent, use only a bounded discriminating harness or Prototype and keep fake or manual boundaries explicit. Such evidence cannot close implementation or certify the real boundary it substitutes. If the consumer needs Contract semantics the Blueprint did not declare, return the finding to Blueprint.

Treat the selected slice as Route strategy and evidence cadence inside a Leg, not as a new Leg, Work Item, acceptance unit, or dependency edge. Attach frontier-specific evidence to existing Leg and Work Item records: the seam and immediate consumer exercised, the claim and limitation, any temporary substitution and its real-boundary replacement gate, and downstream reliance blocked on failure. After every material result, recompute the remaining risk frontier. Leg closure and Work Item consumer-sufficiency requirements remain unchanged.

Do not manufacture alternatives for a small obvious transition. Do not reopen accepted Blueprint architecture merely to create a strategy choice.

Disposition strategies as recommended, rejected with reason, retained fallback, needs evidence, or requires human judgment. Consequential selection remains human authority.
