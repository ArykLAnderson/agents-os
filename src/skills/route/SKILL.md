# Route

Own one resumable delivery-design session and its current candidate realization graph. Turn an accepted Blueprint into a Deliver-ready transition without redefining behavior, architecture, or implementation truth.

## Start Or Resume

Use an explicit Casebook workspace root when present; otherwise use `.casebook/` in the current project. Create or resume `routes/<route-id>/route.md` using [State](references/state.md).

Admit work only when:

- the destination and accepted Blueprint revision are explicit;
- current terrain is current enough to identify material transition deltas;
- unresolved behavior returns to Frame and unresolved responsibility/Contract design returns to Blueprint;
- implementation and migration remain unauthorized unless separately granted.

Bind the accepted Blueprint, current terrain, applicable Cases/evidence, and explicit deferrals. Route references their authority; it does not copy or absorb it.

## Relentless Loop

Continue model-resolvable local, non-destructive delivery design without asking permission merely to advance. Choose the smallest operation fitting current uncertainty:

1. [Strategies](references/strategies.md) — compare materially different realization shapes when transition risk warrants it.
2. [Legs](references/legs.md) — form coherent, integrated, independently verifiable behavioral movements.
3. [Work Items](references/work-items.md) — identify bounded contributions needed inside each Leg.
4. [Dependencies](references/dependencies.md) — name real delivery prerequisites and expose hidden convergence work.
5. [Coverage](references/coverage.md) — map every material current-to-Blueprint delta, temporary mechanism, and evidence obligation.
6. [Review](references/review.md) — challenge Route fidelity, vertical closure, intermediate states, reversibility, and Deliver readiness.

These are condition-selected operations, not mandatory sequential phases. New findings route by meaning:

- missing/contradictory behavior → Frame or human reconciliation;
- bad responsibility/module/Contract → Blueprint;
- bad transition, Leg, dependency, migration, compatibility, or evidence strategy → Route;
- local implementation defect → Deliver after Route acceptance.

Use `prototype` for the smallest discriminating test when realization materially depends on uncertain migration, compatibility, platform, external mutation, failure recovery, or rollback behavior. Happy-path contact is insufficient when failure behavior determines the Route.

## Frame Handoff And Persistence Cadence

An isolated delivery-local choice may remain in Route. Multiple related human questions, consequential trade-offs, missing behavior, or authority-dependent judgments form a bounded Frame objective. Frame owns the dependency-ordered interview; accumulate answers without rewriting Route/Cases after each turn, then reconcile the accepted batch at a natural boundary and resume Route.

## Human Decision Card

When human judgment is required, default to one bounded card:

- **Context:** one or two sentences.
- **Definitions:** only unfamiliar terms needed to answer.
- **Decision:** one explicit delivery trade-off.
- **Recommendation:** strongest supported option.
- **Consequences:** one to three decisive effects.
- **Question:** one answerable prompt.

Keep detailed graphs, evidence, and rationale in Route artifacts. Expand only when the decision cannot be made responsibly from the card or the user requests depth.

## Authority And Stops

Proceed autonomously through terrain comparison, strategy modeling, graph formation, dependency analysis, evidence planning, prototypes, coverage, and review when local and non-destructive.

Confirm before:

- changing accepted behavior or Blueprint responsibility/Contracts;
- selecting a consequential strategy or trade-off on the human's behalf;
- promoting reviewer advice into governing Route semantics, package membership, acceptance criteria, or proof protocols without accepted authority;
- materially expanding scope;
- performing credentialed, shared-resource, costly, destructive, deployed, or externally mutating work;
- authorizing implementation, migration, deployment, or delivery;
- marking Route accepted.

Reviewer outputs are advisory Findings. Severity and repeated agreement do not confer authority.

## Completion

Recommend acceptance only when:

- current terrain and accepted Blueprint revision are identified;
- consequential strategies were compared;
- every Leg closes one meaningful integrated behavioral movement;
- Work Items are bounded and collectively sufficient to close their Leg; Work Items exporting seams prove consumer sufficiency, and every Work Item names verification independent from its writer;
- the Route and each Leg's Work Item graph are acyclic, and every edge names a real prerequisite;
- current-to-Blueprint transition coverage is complete;
- each Leg names evidence or risk retired;
- intermediate states remain coherent;
- temporary mechanisms have cleanup/retirement dispositions;
- material platform assumptions receive early reality contact;
- findings have correct semantic destinations;
- Deliver can execute without inventing architecture, strategy, dependency meaning, or acceptance evidence.

The human explicitly accepts the current Route revision, consequential transition trade-offs, and deferrals. Route may instead end `rejected`, `abandoned`, or `superseded`.

Route acceptance authorizes no implementation by itself. Deliver begins only under the configured execution and authorization workflow.
