# Genre Review Lens

Assess whether the artifact fulfills the declared genre and reader contract without requiring the reader to reconstruct the Case.

For an RFC decision brief, verify the decision or ask appears first, scope is bounded, supporting evidence and trade-offs are sufficient, risks and open gaps are visible, and the requested action is explicit. When policy is already accepted, verify that the artifact is a decision record with no Case-backed action. A supplied exercise review action may be recorded as external context, but cannot be reviewed as Case-supported or presented as publication-ready authority. Flag a genre failure when a reader must reconstruct the recommendation from sources or when background crowds out the action.

For an evidence-heavy research report, verify that it states the reader action, presents a qualified conclusion, separates observation from accepted policy, exposes decisive and conflicting evidence, gives material limitations appropriate prominence, and ends with a usable decision boundary. Flag chronology dumps, unqualified recommendations, buried caveats, and evidence lists that never explain why they matter.

Do not require a generic RFC outline. Recommend only changes that make the declared reader action possible without adding Case meaning. Record each finding with severity, locator, reader impact, and a proposed non-semantic edit when one exists. A repair that alters authority, confidence, scope, evidence, or reader action must route to `case-reconcile`.
