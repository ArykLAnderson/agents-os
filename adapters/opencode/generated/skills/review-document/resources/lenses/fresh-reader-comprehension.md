# Fresh Reader Comprehension Lens

Simulate the named reader using only a frozen named artifact revision and its stated reader contract, not the Case, trace, sources, review notes, or author instructions. Record the artifact locator or digest and the excluded inputs so the result can be inspected as isolated comprehension evidence. This is comprehension evidence, not stakeholder approval, authority, or a substitute for fidelity review.

Record concise answers to these prompts:

1. What action is the reader being asked to take or avoid?
2. What is the recommendation, and how qualified is it?
3. Which observation supports the recommendation, and who selected the policy direction?
4. What contradictory or weak-authority claim should not be treated as current policy?
5. What limitation or unresolved question constrains the action?

Also record whether the reader can identify the current direction or unresolved decision, material risk or caveat, and next owner or approval boundary. Record a misunderstanding when the artifact makes an unsupported decision, hides a caveat needed for the action, blurs evidence with authority, or leaves the next step unclear, with the artifact anchor that caused it. Keep the result short: outcome, confidence, answers, misunderstanding signals, and the smallest author-facing change needed. State the simulation's confidence limit: it cannot establish real reader comprehension, approval, or operational acceptance.
